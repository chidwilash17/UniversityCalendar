Ambedkar Jayanti

Ambedkar Jayanti or Bhim Jayanti is observed on 14 April to commemorate the memory of Dr. B. R. Ambedkar, an Indian jurist, economist, social reformer and the principal architect of constitution of India. The observance marks the birthday of Ambedkar, who was born on 14 April 1891. His birthday is also referred to as 'Equality Day' by many in India. In 2016, The Government of India declared 14 April as Rashtriya Samarasata Diwas (National Harmony Day) to honor Ambedkar's legacy and contributions.

Dr. Bhimrao Ramji Ambedkar was a visionary leader who dedicated his life to eradicating social inequality in India. As India's first Law Minister and the chairman of the drafting committee of the Constitution, he played a pivotal role in shaping modern India. His work focused on social justice, equality, and the rights of marginalized communities, particularly the Dalits. Ambedkar was also a prolific scholar, having earned multiple doctorates from Columbia University and the London School of Economics.

On Ambedkar Jayanti, processions, memorial speeches, and cultural programs are organized across India. People visit his statues and memorials to pay homage, with the largest gatherings at the Chaitya Bhoomi in Mumbai and Deeksha Bhoomi in Nagpur. Educational institutions conduct special events highlighting his contributions. The day serves as a reminder of his ideals of social justice, equality, and human rights, inspiring millions to continue working towards an equitable society.

Celebrated annually on April 14th
