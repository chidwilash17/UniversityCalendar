Mahavir Swami Jayanti

Mahavir Janma Kalyanak is one of the most important religious festivals in Jainism. It celebrates the birth of Mahavira, the twenty-fourth and last Tirthankara (supreme preacher) of present Avasarpiá¹‡Ä«. On the Gregorian calendar, the holiday occurs either in March or April.

Born in the early part of the 6th century BCE, Mahavira was born into royalty as the son of King Siddhartha and Queen Trishala. According to Jain texts, Mahavira was born at Kundalagrama (present-day Kundalpur in Bihar, India). The festival is marked with prayers, fasting, and recitations of Jain texts in temples. Devotees bring offerings to temples and meditate. The most important event is the procession where an image of Mahavira is carried out on a chariot, with devotees singing and dancing in the streets.

Mahavira's teachings focused on non-violence (ahimsa), truthfulness (satya), non-stealing (asteya), chastity (brahmacharya), and non-attachment (aparigraha). These principles form the core of Jain philosophy. On Mahavir Jayanti, Jains engage in charitable acts, visit sacred sites, and reflect on the path to liberation that Mahavira taught. Temples are decorated with flags, and lectures are held to preach the path of virtue as defined by Jainism.

Celebrated annually in March/April (date varies)
