Diwali Celebration

Diwali, the festival of lights, is one of the most popular Hindu festivals celebrated across India and the world. Marking the victory of light over darkness and good over evil, Diwali is observed on the new moon night (Amavasya) in the Hindu month of Kartik (October/November).

The festival spans five days, beginning with Dhanteras and culminating with Bhai Dooj. Homes are decorated with diyas (oil lamps), rangoli designs, and colorful lights. Families perform Lakshmi Puja for prosperity and exchange sweets and gifts. The night sky lights up with fireworks displays across cities and villages.

Diwali also marks the return of Lord Rama to Ayodhya after 14 years of exile. In different regions, it commemorates various legends including the victory of Lord Krishna over Narakasura. The festival promotes unity, cleanliness, and the renewal of relationships.

Celebrated in Kartik month (October/November)
