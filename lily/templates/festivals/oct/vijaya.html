Vijayadashami (Dussehra)

Vijayadashami, also known as Dussehra, is a major Hindu festival celebrated at the end of Navratri every year. It falls on the tenth day of the bright half (Shukla Paksha) of the month Ashvin (September-October). The festival commemorates Lord Rama's victory over Ravana and Goddess Durga's victory over Mahishasura, symbolizing the triumph of good over evil.

In northern India, the festival is marked by Ramlila performances and the burning of huge effigies of Ravana, Kumbhakarna, and Meghnad. In southern and eastern India, it's celebrated as the day Goddess Durga killed Mahishasura and is marked by the immersion of Durga idols. The day is considered auspicious for starting new ventures and students traditionally begin their learning journey on this day in the Vidyarambham ceremony.

Celebrations include visiting temples, exchanging sweets, and family gatherings. In many regions, people worship weapons and tools as part of Ayudha Puja. The festival also marks the end of the rainy season and beginning of the harvest season in many parts of India.

Celebrated on the 10th day of Ashvin month (September-October)
