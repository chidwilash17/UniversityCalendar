Gandhi Jayanti

Gandhi Jayanti is celebrated annually on 2nd October to mark the birth anniversary of Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi, the Father of the Nation. This national festival honors Gandhi's contributions to India's freedom struggle and his philosophy of truth (Satya) and non-violence (Ahimsa).

The day is observed with prayer services, tributes, and cultural events across India. The main ceremony takes place at Gandhi's memorial Raj Ghat in New Delhi, where the President and Prime Minister pay homage. Schools and institutions conduct special assemblies featuring Gandhi's favorite bhajan "Raghupati Raghav Raja Ram".

The United Nations declared 2nd October as the International Day of Non-Violence in 2007. On this day, people promote Gandhi's teachings through cleanliness drives (Swachh Bharat Abhiyan), spinning charkhas, and exhibitions showcasing his life. The three national institutions founded by Gandhi - Sabarmati Ashram, Sevagram Ashram, and Phoenix Settlement - hold special commemorations.
