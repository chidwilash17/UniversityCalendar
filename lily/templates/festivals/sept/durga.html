Durga Ashtami

Durga Ashtami, also known as Maha Ashtami, is one of the most significant days during the Navratri festival. It falls on the eighth day of the bright lunar fortnight in the month of Ashwin (September-October). This day commemorates the victory of Goddess Durga over the buffalo demon Mahishasura, symbolizing the triumph of good over evil.

On this day, devotees perform special pujas like the Astra Puja (worship of weapons) as it's believed Goddess Durga received her weapons on this day. In many regions, young unmarried girls are worshipped as manifestations of the goddess in a ritual called Kanya Puja. Temples and pandals witness huge gatherings of devotees offering prayers and performing aarti.

The celebrations include traditional dances like Garba and Dandiya in western India, while in eastern India, it's part of the grand Durga Puja festivities. Devotees observe fasting and offer red-colored flowers and sweets to the goddess. The evening Sandhi Puja, performed at the juncture of Ashtami and Navami, is considered especially auspicious when 108 lamps are lit.

Celebrated on the 8th day of Navratri (Ashwin month)
