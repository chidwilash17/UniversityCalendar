Teachers' Day

Teachers' Day is celebrated annually on September 5th in India to honor the birth anniversary of Dr. Sarvepalli Radhakrishnan, the second President of India and a renowned teacher. This day recognizes the valuable contributions of teachers in shaping students' lives and the future of the nation.

Schools and colleges across India celebrate this day with special programs where students perform cultural activities, speeches, and skits to express their gratitude. Senior students often take on teaching roles for junior classes, giving teachers a day of rest. Many institutions award outstanding teachers and organize felicitation ceremonies.

The day emphasizes the importance of teachers in society and the teaching profession. Students present flowers, cards, and gifts to their teachers as tokens of appreciation. Dr. Radhakrishnan himself suggested that instead of celebrating his birthday, the day should be observed as Teachers' Day to honor all teachers - a tradition that continues today.
