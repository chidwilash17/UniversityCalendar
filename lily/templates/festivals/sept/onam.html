Onam Festival

Celebrating Kerala's Harvest Festival

Onam

Onam is the annual harvest festival of Kerala celebrated with great enthusiasm. It commemorates the homecoming of the legendary King Mahabali and marks the Malayalam New Year. The festival falls in the month of Chingam (August-September) and lasts for ten days, with the most important day being Thiru Onam.

The celebrations include elaborate feasts (Onasadya), snake boat races (Vallamkali), intricate flower carpets (Pookalam), traditional dance performances like Kathakali and Pulikali (tiger dance), and cultural events. The Onasadya, served on banana leaves, typically consists of 26-28 dishes including rice, sambar, avial, thoran, payasam, and other traditional delicacies.

Onam is celebrated by people of all communities in Kerala. The government organizes tourism week with spectacular processions in Thiruvananthapuram. Traditional games like Onakalikal (tug-of-war, archery) and competitions for best Pookalam add to the festive spirit. The festival showcases Kerala's rich cultural heritage and promotes unity in diversity.

Celebrated in Chingam month (August-September)
