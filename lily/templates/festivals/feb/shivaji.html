Shiv Jayanti

Shiv Jayanti, also known as Shivaji festival, is a festival and public holiday of the Indian state of Maharashtra. This festival is celebrated on February 19, commemorating the birth anniversary of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, the founder of the Maratha Empire and the first Chhatrapati of the Marathas. He established Hindavi Swarajya (Hindavƒ´ SvarƒÅjya; "Self-Rule of the hindavi people").

Shivaji Maharaj was a legendary warrior king known for his exceptional military strategies, progressive administration, and deep respect for all religions. He built a strong navy to protect the Konkan coast and established a well-structured administrative system with the Ashta Pradhan council. His coronation in 1674 at Raigad Fort marked the formal establishment of the Maratha Empire.

The celebrations include processions, cultural programs, and speeches highlighting Shivaji Maharaj's life and values. Many people visit his forts like Raigad, Sinhagad, and Pratapgad on this day. The festival inspires patriotism and reminds people of Shivaji Maharaj's ideals of good governance, religious tolerance, and women's empowerment.

Celebrated annually on February 19th
