Indian Independence Day

Independence Day is celebrated annually on 15th August as a national holiday in India commemorating the nation's independence from the United Kingdom on 15 August 1947. This historic day marks the end of British rule in India and the birth of a free and independent Indian nation.

The main celebration takes place in New Delhi, where the Prime Minister hoists the national flag at the Red Fort and delivers a nationally televised speech. Similar flag-hoisting ceremonies are held in state capitals, cities, towns, and villages across the country. The day is observed with parades, cultural events, and the singing of patriotic songs.

The tricolor Indian flag is prominently displayed throughout the country on this day. Citizens adorn their homes, vehicles, and workplaces with flags and patriotic decorations. Schools and colleges organize cultural programs, debates, and competitions. The celebration serves as a reminder of the sacrifices made by freedom fighters and reinforces the values of unity, diversity, and national pride.
