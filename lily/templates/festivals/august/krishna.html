Krishna Janmashtami

Krishna Janmashtami, also known simply as Janmashtami or Gokulashtami, is an annual Hindu festival that celebrates the birth of Krishna, the eighth avatar of Vishnu. It is observed according to the Hindu lunisolar calendar, on the eighth day (Ashtami) of the Krishna Paksha (dark fortnight) in Shravana or Bhadrapad (depending on whether the calendar chooses the new moon or full moon day as the last day of the month).

The festival is particularly celebrated in Mathura and Vrindavan, where Krishna is believed to have been born and spent his childhood respectively. Major celebrations include devotional songs, dance-drama enactments of Krishna's life (Rasa Lila), fasting until midnight (the time of Krishna's birth), and the Dahi Handi ritual where teams form human pyramids to break pots of curd suspended high above the ground, reenacting Krishna's childhood antics of stealing butter.

Temples and homes are beautifully decorated for the occasion. Baby Krishna idols are bathed and placed in cradles at midnight, marking the moment of his birth. Devotees prepare elaborate feasts of fruits, sweets, and other vegetarian delicacies as offerings. The festival inspires spiritual reflection on Krishna's teachings in the Bhagavad Gita and celebrates his divine play (leela) as a child and youth.

Celebrated on the 8th day of Krishna Paksha in Bhadrapada month
