Raksha Bandhan

Raksha Bandhan, commonly known as Rakhi, is an annual Hindu festival that celebrates the love and duty between brothers and sisters. The central ceremony involves the sister tying a decorative thread (rakhi) around her brother's wrist, symbolizing her love and prayers for his well-being, and the brother's lifelong vow to protect her.

The festival falls on the full moon day of the Hindu month of Shravana (typically July or August). The word "Raksha" means protection, while "Bandhan" means bond. Beyond biological siblings, the festival has expanded to include cousins, sister-like figures, and even close friends in some communities.

Traditional celebrations include sisters preparing the rakhi thali with the sacred thread, sweets, roli (vermilion powder), and rice. After performing aarti and tying the rakhi, brothers give gifts and promise protection. The festival has evolved to include long-distance rakhi sending and digital celebrations for siblings separated by distance.

Celebrated on the full moon day of Shravana month (July-August)
