Ganesh Chaturthi

Ganesh Chaturthi, also known as Vinayaka Chaturthi, is a Hindu festival celebrating the birth of Lord Ganesha, the elephant-headed god of wisdom and prosperity. The festival is observed in the Hindu month of Bhadrapada (August-September) and lasts for 10 days, ending on Anant Chaturdashi.

The festival begins with the installation of beautifully crafted Ganesha clay idols in homes and public pandals. The celebrations include daily prayers (aartis), devotional songs, dance performances, and offerings of modak (sweet dumplings), Ganesha's favorite food. Cultural events and community feasts are organized throughout the festival period.

The festival culminates with the immersion (Visarjan) of the idols in water bodies, accompanied by grand processions with music and dancing. In Maharashtra, the festival is celebrated with great enthusiasm, particularly in Mumbai and Pune. Environmental awareness has led to the promotion of eco-friendly clay idols and natural colors for the immersion process.

Celebrated in Bhadrapada month (August-September)
