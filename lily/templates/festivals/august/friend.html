Friendship Day

Friendship Day is celebrated annually on the first Sunday of August in many countries, honoring the special bond between friends. The tradition began in the United States in 1935 and has since spread worldwide. On this day, people celebrate their friendships by exchanging gifts, cards, and friendship bands, often tied on wrists as a symbol of their bond.

The day encourages people to cherish their friendships and make new friends. Popular activities include parties, outings with friends, and social media posts celebrating friendships. Many schools and colleges organize special events where students can appreciate their friends. The iconic friendship band - typically made of threads in various colors - has become a universal symbol of this day.

In 2011, the United Nations officially recognized July 30 as International Friendship Day, emphasizing friendship between peoples, countries, cultures and individuals to inspire peace efforts. However, many countries continue to celebrate on the first Sunday of August. The day reminds us of the importance of friends in our lives and encourages us to nurture these valuable relationships.
