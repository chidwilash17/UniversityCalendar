Kabirdas Jayanti

Kabir Jayanti celebrates the birth of the 15th-century Indian mystic poet and saint Kabir Das. Observed on the full moon day of Jyeshtha month (May-June) in the Hindu calendar, this day honors the life and teachings of one of India's most revered spiritual figures.

Kabir's origins are shrouded in legend. Some believe he was born to Muslim parents, while others hold that he miraculously appeared on a lotus flower in the sacred Lahartara lake near Varanasi. This divine manifestation is commemorated at the Kabirpanth on Lahartara lake, where devotees gather annually.

Kabir's teachings transcended religious boundaries, blending elements of Hindu Bhakti and Islamic Sufi traditions. His dohas (couplets) continue to inspire millions with their message of unity, devotion, and social equality. Celebrations include recitations of his poetry, devotional singing, and spiritual discourses.

Celebrated on Jyeshtha Purnima (Full Moon Day)
