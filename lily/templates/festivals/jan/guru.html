Guru Govind Singh Jayanti

Guru Gobind Singh Jayanti commemorates the birth anniversary of Guru Gobind Singh Ji, the tenth Sikh Guru and the founder of the Khalsa Panth. Born on December 22, 1666 (according to the Gregorian calendar), this day is celebrated with great fervor by Sikh communities worldwide.

Guru Gobind Singh Ji was a spiritual master, warrior, poet, and philosopher. He established the Khalsa in 1699, initiating the Five Ks tradition and giving Sikhs a distinct identity. He also completed the Guru Granth Sahib, the holy scripture of Sikhism, and declared it the eternal Guru of Sikhs.

Celebrations include religious processions (Nagar Kirtan), reading of the Guru Granth Sahib, and community meals (Langar). Gurdwaras are decorated with lights and flowers, and special prayers are offered. Though it's an optional holiday in India, many Sikhs take the day off to participate in religious activities.

Celebrated annually on January 6th (varies by lunar calendar)
