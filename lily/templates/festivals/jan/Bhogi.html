Bhogi Festival

Bhogi is the first day of the four-day Pongal festival, celebrated widely in Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, and Maharashtra. It falls on the last day of the AgrahƒÅya·πáa or MƒÅrga≈õƒ´r·π£a month of the Hindu Solar Calendar, which is January 13th by the Gregorian calendar.

The festival marks the beginning of the harvest season and is dedicated to Lord Indra, the god of rains. On this day, people discard old and derelict things and concentrate on new things causing change or transformation.

A special puja is performed before the cutting of paddy. Farmers worship the sun and earth by anointing their ploughs and sickles with sandalwood paste, ready to cut the newly ready paddy. The most significant ritual of Bhogi is the burning of useless household articles in a bonfire made of wood and cow-dung cakes.

Celebrated annually on January 13th
