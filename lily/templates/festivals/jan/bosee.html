Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose Jayanti

Celebrating the birth anniversary of India's fearless freedom fighter

Parakram Diwas (Day of Valour)

Netaji Jayanti or Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose Jayanti, officially known as Parakram Diwas ('Day of Valour'), is celebrated annually on 23 January to commemorate the birthday of the prominent Indian freedom fighter Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose. He played a pivotal role in India's independence movement and was the founder of the Azad Hind Fauj (Indian National Army).

Netaji's famous slogan "Give me blood and I will give you freedom" inspired millions of Indians to join the freedom struggle. As the head of the Azad Hind Government, he established the first independent Indian government on foreign soil and led the Indian National Army in battles against the British.

Celebrations include flag-hoisting ceremonies, cultural programs, and tributes at Netaji's statues across the country. The day serves as a reminder of his courage, leadership, and unwavering commitment to India's independence.

Celebrated annually on January 23rd
