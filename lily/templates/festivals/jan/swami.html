Swami Vivekananda Jayanti

Celebrating National Youth Day

NATIONAL YOUTH DAY

National Youth Day, also known as Vivekananda Jayanti, is celebrated on 12 January, being the birthday of the great Hindu monk, Swami Vivekananda. In 1984, the Government of India declared this day as National Youth Day, and since 1985 the event has been celebrated across India every year.

Swami Vivekananda (1863-1902) was a key figure in the introduction of Indian philosophies of Vedanta and Yoga to the Western world. His famous speech at the 1893 World's Parliament of Religions in Chicago introduced Hinduism to America and earned him widespread recognition. He emphasized the importance of education, self-confidence, and national consciousness among Indian youth.

On this day, schools and colleges across India organize processions, speeches, recitations, music, youth conventions, seminars, yoga asanas, presentations, and competitions in essay-writing, recitations, and sports. The day serves as an inspiration for Indian youth to follow Vivekananda's teachings of strength, fearlessness, and service to humanity.

Celebrated annually on January 12th
