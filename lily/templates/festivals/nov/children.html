Children's Day

Children's Day is celebrated annually on November 14th in India to mark the birth anniversary of India's first Prime Minister, Pandit Jawaharlal Nehru, who was fondly called Chacha Nehru. This day emphasizes the importance of nurturing and educating children, recognizing them as the future of the nation.

Schools and educational institutions organize special events including cultural programs, competitions, and fun activities. Teachers often perform skits and dances for students, reversing traditional roles. Many organizations conduct charity events and distribute gifts to underprivileged children.

The day focuses on children's rights, education, and welfare. Special assemblies are held where students participate in speeches and performances. Government and NGOs launch new child welfare initiatives on this day. It's a day filled with games, sweets, and celebrations dedicated to honoring childhood.

Celebrated annually on November 14th
